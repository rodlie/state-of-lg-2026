# Inkscape project notes for Libre Graphic Meeting 2026

Inkscape is an open source vector editor, working on the SVG standard (Scalable Vector Graphics). The project started in 2003 and is still in excellent health 22 years later, thanks to its very active community of users, contributors and developers.

New major versions are released every 1 or 2 years since 2019, with minor releases every 6 months or so.

## For the 2025-2026 period

### Inkscape core

- We released v.1.4.2 in May 2025, the last version is currently 1.4.3 available since Dec. 2025. A v1.4.4 is on the way and is currently planned for May.
- 1.4.x relases focused on bugfixes (about 200+), many improvements around import/export file formats: fix JPEG export issue on Windows, various fixes for PDF, improved support for Affinity Designer and initial support for Vectornator/Linearity Curve files, and many more! Noticeable work has been done on translations and documentation.

### Project, community and infrastructure

- Inkscape took part in the IndiaFOSS 2025 conference. We had a booth where visitors could try Inkscape, learn more about the difference between vector and raster images and even participate in drawing a community conference doodle!
- Inkscape user, designer and short film artist Jorge del Campo Andrade used Inkscape in his process of creating the shortfilm "Ladrón de Flores", and talked to us about his experience of taking part in the Peru-based international children's film festival "Mi primer festival".
-  Work on updating our website continues, as well as our endeavours to move our chat from Rocket.chat to Matrix.
- Donations: we are working on expanding donations options to include Stripe, and to publish a "donation" version on Steam, etc.
- Like many FLOSS projects, we have concerns about AI-driven developments: maintaining quality and sustainability in the Inkscape codebase and the whole community opens many questions. An AI policy is currently being drafted to give a clear frame within the project.

## For Inkscape 1.5 and beyond

Background work continues for version 1.5. In the next major version, a special focus will be on:

- Stabilizing the GTK4 port (Inkscape has been on GTK3 since Inkscape 1.0).
- Support for color spaces, including CMYK rendering and native print-ready PDF export. This involves a huge rework of the internals of Inkscape, much has already been done, work continues.
- Hiring 3 persons (2 C++ devs + 1 issue admin) to give a boost to bugfixing for the big release.
- UI/UX improvements: the UX + Developer teams are working hard to redesign panels, tools, iconset, etc. for next major version. Some refactoring is also in the works for 1.5 for grids, autosave/recent files, and multipage (a quite fresh feature in Inkscape, intended to evolve into a more standard element, e.g. for PDF multipage compatibility).
